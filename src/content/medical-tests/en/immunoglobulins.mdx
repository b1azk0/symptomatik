  - heading: "What Are Immunoglobulins and How Do You Interpret Their Results?"
    body: "Immunoglobulins are proteins produced by plasma cells that play a key role in identifying and neutralising pathogens such as bacteria and viruses. There are several classes — IgA, IgG, IgM, IgE, and IgD — each performing different functions in the immune response. For example, IgG is the most abundant immunoglobulin in serum and plays a key role in long-term protection against infection, while IgE is associated mainly with allergic reactions. Analysing levels of individual immunoglobulin types can help detect immune deficiencies, chronic infections, autoimmune diseases, as well as allergies and hypersensitivities.\n\nInterpreting immunoglobulin results requires consideration of reference ranges, which can vary by patient age, sex, and assay method. Significant deviations from these norms may indicate health issues requiring further workup. For example, elevated IgM may suggest acute infection, while low IgA may be associated with congenital immune deficiency. Online interpretation services allow for quick information about potential disorders, but consultation with a clinician is always recommended to confirm diagnosis and establish an appropriate treatment plan."
