    body: "Low testosterone (hypogonadism) can result from aging, testicular or pituitary disease, certain medications, chronic illnesses, obesity, or genetic conditions; it commonly causes low libido, erectile dysfunction, fatigue, reduced muscle mass and bone density, weight gain, cognitive changes, and mood disturbances. Diagnosis combines symptoms with low total or free testosterone on morning blood tests and sometimes pituitary imaging or fertility evaluation. Treatment depends on cause and goals: lifestyle changes, treating underlying disease, and testosterone replacement therapy (with monitoring and fertility-sparing options when needed) or alternative hormone/assisted-reproduction strategies for men desiring fertility."

Diagnosis requires measurement of total and free testosterone with appropriate timing, assessment of other androgens and gonadotropins, imaging when indicated, and evaluation of medication history and comorbidities. Management targets the underlying cause, may include stopping exogenous androgens, hormonal therapies (e.g., oral contraceptives, anti-androgens), or surgery for tumors, and balances symptom control with fertility and cardiovascular/metabolic risk monitoring."
