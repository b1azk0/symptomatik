    body: "Burnout is a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged workplace stress. It's characterized by depersonalization, reduced personal accomplishment, and emotional exhaustion. Unlike temporary work stress, burnout develops gradually and requires comprehensive intervention.   - heading: "CBI Dimensions: Personal, Work-Related, and Client-Related"
    body: "The CBI measures personal burnout (physical and psychological fatigue), work-related burnout (exhaustion related to work demands), and client-related burnout (loss of empathy toward those served). Personal burnout affects energy and emotional resources. Work-related burnout involves feeling overwhelmed by job demands. Client-related burnout is common in helping professions. Understanding these dimensions helps identify specific intervention needs."

    body: "Burnout symptoms include chronic fatigue, cynicism, reduced effectiveness, emotional detachment, and physical health problems. Work performance and motivation decline.
