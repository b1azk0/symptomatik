    body: "Diagnosis of postpartum depression rests on detailed clinical assessment, including medical history, emotional and behavioural symptoms, and psychological evaluation. The key is identifying symptoms such as persistent sadness, loss of interest in everyday activities, fatigue, sleep disturbances, and difficulty bonding with the baby. Diagnostic tools such as the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) can be used to assess symptom severity. The EPDS is a 10-item self-report questionnaire; total scores range from 0 to 30, with scores of 10 or higher commonly used as a threshold for further clinical assessment, and scores of 13 or higher more strongly indicating probable depression. Item 10 — assessing self-harm thoughts — requires immediate clinical attention regardless of the total score. It is also important to rule out other medical conditions that can mimic or co-occur with depression, and to take into account risk factors such as a personal history of depression. Diagnosis should be carried out by a qualified mental health specialist to ensure appropriate support and treatment."
