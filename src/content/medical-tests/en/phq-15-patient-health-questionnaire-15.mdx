    body: "Health questionnaires are standardized tools used to screen for, assess, and monitor physical and mental health by collecting patient-reported symptoms, functioning, and risk factors in a consistent format. They range from brief screening instruments that flag possible issues to comprehensive diagnostic or outcome measures used for treatment planning and tracking progress. Validity and reliability determine how confidently results can inform clinical decisions, so clinicians choose instruments appropriate to the setting, purpose, and population. When interpreted alongside history and examination, questionnaire results help prioritize further assessment, guide diagnosis and care plans, and support outcome measurement, while requiring secure collection and storage to protect patient privacy."

    body: "Somatic symptom disorder should be suspected when patients report multiple, persistent (>6 months) or distressing physical symptoms that cause significant functional impairment or excessive health-related thoughts/behaviors disproportionate to identifiable medical findings; use standardized screens (eg, PHQ-15 for somatic burden, PHQ-9/GAD-7 for comorbid mood/anxiety) at baseline and follow-up to quantify severity and monitor response, but never substitute questionnaires for clinical assessment or urgent evaluation of red flags (fever, focal neuro deficits, weight loss, bleeding, severe pain, systemic signs).
