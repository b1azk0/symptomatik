  - heading: "Understanding Mood Disorders"
    body: "Understanding mood disorders means recognizing that conditions like major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder, dysthymia (persistent depressive disorder), and cyclothymia involve persistent disturbances in mood, energy, sleep, concentration, and daily functioning. Symptoms can range from prolonged sadness, loss of interest, and fatigue to extreme mood swings, irritability, and risky behavior; causes often combine genetic, biological, psychological, and environmental factors. Early screening and accurate assessment are crucial because they clarify severity, differentiate among disorder types, and guide effective treatment planning—typically a combination of psychotherapy, medication, lifestyle changes, and social support—so individuals receive timely, tailored care that reduces symptom duration and improves long-term outcomes."
  - heading: "MDQ Screening Questions and Symptoms"
    body: "The MDQ screening tool asks about periods of elevated mood, increased energy, decreased need for sleep, and risky behaviors. These symptoms help clinicians identify manic or hypomanic episodes. The questionnaire also assesses the functional impact and duration of mood episodes. Understanding which symptoms you experience is important for accurate diagnosis."
  - heading: "Differences Between Bipolar Disorder and Depression"
    body: "The key distinction between bipolar disorder and unipolar depression is the presence of manic or hypomanic episodes. While depression involves persistent low mood, bipolar disorder includes periods of abnormally elevated mood and energy. This difference is crucial because treating bipolar depression with standard antidepressants alone can trigger mood episodes. The MDQ helps identify these distinct patterns."
  - heading: "When Screening Results Warrant Professional Evaluation"
    body: "If your MDQ screening suggests possible bipolar disorder, a comprehensive psychiatric evaluation is essential. A mental health professional will conduct detailed interviews, consider family history, and may recommend mood tracking. It's important not to self-diagnose based on screening results alone, as proper diagnosis requires clinical assessment."
  - heading: "Treatment and Management of Mood Disorders"
    body: "Bipolar disorder treatment typically involves mood-stabilizing medications combined with psychotherapy. Approaches such as cognitive-behavioral therapy and family therapy are evidence-based treatments. Lifestyle management including consistent sleep patterns, stress reduction, and social support are critical components of long-term management. Working with a psychiatrist experienced in bipolar disorder is important for optimal treatment outcomes."
