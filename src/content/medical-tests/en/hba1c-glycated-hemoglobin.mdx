h1Text: "HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin) is a blood test that reflects average blood glucose levels over the past 2–3 months and is a key tool for diagnosing and monitoring diabetes and prediabetes. Typical reference ranges are about 4.0–5.6% for normal, 5.7–6.4% for prediabetes, and ≥6.5% for diabetes, though targets and interpretation vary by age, pregnancy status, comorbidities, and individual treatment goals. Results can be affected by hemoglobin variants, anemia, recent transfusions, and certain medical conditions or medications, so clinicians interpret values alongside clinical context and other glucose measurements. Understanding what different HbA1c levels mean helps guide diagnosis, risk assessment, and personalized management of blood sugar."
