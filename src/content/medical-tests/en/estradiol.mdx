    body: "Estradiol is the primary circulating estrogen produced mainly by the ovaries (and in smaller amounts by adipose tissue and the testes) that regulates reproductive function, menstrual cycles, bone density, cardiovascular health, and secondary sex characteristics; measuring estradiol in blood helps evaluate ovarian reserve and function, fertility and menstrual irregularities, menopausal status, pregnancy-related changes, and certain endocrine disorders, but values must be interpreted against age, sex, menstrual phase, pregnancy status, and hormone therapy—abnormal results often prompt repeat testing, imaging, or specialist referral for diagnosis and management."

  - heading: "Low Estradiol: Symptoms & Health Effects"
    body: "Low estradiol commonly causes menstrual irregularities or amenorrhea, hot flashes and night sweats, vaginal dryness and dyspareunia, reduced libido and fertility, mood changes (irritability, depression, anxiety), fatigue and sleep disturbance, and long-term effects on bone density (osteopenia/osteoporosis) with increased fracture risk and adverse lipid–cardiovascular changes; in men low estradiol may contribute to decreased libido and bone loss."
  - heading: "High Estradiol: Causes & Management"
    body: "High estradiol can result from ovarian hyperstimulation, estrogen-producing tumors (ovarian or adrenal), exogenous estrogen therapy, obesity (increased aromatization), polycystic ovary syndrome, liver disease, or certain medications; symptoms may include abnormal uterine bleeding, breast tenderness, mood changes, and thromboembolic risk. Management depends on the cause and may include adjusting or stopping estrogen therapy, treating underlying tumors or liver disease, weight loss, ovulation suppression with combined hormonal contraception or GnRH analogs, and referral to endocrinology or gynecology for targeted testing (serial estradiol, imaging) and long-term risk mitigation."
