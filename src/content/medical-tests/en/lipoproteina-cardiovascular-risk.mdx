Unlike standard cholesterol, which responds to diet and exercise, Lp(a) levels are determined at birth and remain relatively stable throughout life. High Lp(a) can contribute to atherosclerosis, blood clot formation, and inflammatory processes in blood vessels. Individuals with elevated Lp(a), especially when combined with other risk factors, require more aggressive cardiovascular prevention strategies."

    body: "Elevated Lipoprotein(a) is primarily determined by genetic inheritance rather than lifestyle factors. High Lp(a) contributes to cardiovascular disease through multiple mechanisms including atherosclerosis acceleration, thrombosis (blood clot) promotion, and inflammatory effects on blood vessels. Approximately 20% of the population has elevated Lp(a), making it an important but often overlooked risk factor."
