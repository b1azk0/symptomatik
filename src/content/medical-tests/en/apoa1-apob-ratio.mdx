    body: "Apolipoprotein A1 is the primary structural protein of HDL cholesterol, often called 'good cholesterol' due to its protective cardiovascular effects. Apolipoprotein B is the structural protein of LDL and other potentially harmful lipid particles. By comparing these two proteins, the ApoA1/ApoB ratio provides insight into the proportion of protective versus atherogenic particles in your bloodstream, offering a more accurate cardiovascular risk assessment than total cholesterol alone."

    body: "Normal ApoA1/ApoB ratios vary by gender and age, but a ratio above 1.0 is generally considered favorable for cardiovascular health.
