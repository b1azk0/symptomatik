  - heading: "How Adult ADHD Screening is Evaluated"
    body: "Evaluation of adult ADHD screening begins with a validated self-report or clinician-administered questionnaire (e.g., ASRS-v1.1, Conners’ Adult ADHD Rating Scales) scored against standardized cutoffs; clinicians then review responses in context of age-of-onset, symptom persistence across settings, and degree of functional impairment. A thorough evaluation cross-checks screening results with clinical interview, collateral history from partners/family or workplace records, and assessment for comorbid conditions (anxiety, depression, substance use, sleep disorders) that can mimic or worsen symptoms. Objective tests (continuous performance tests) or cognitive testing may be used selectively to clarify attention and executive-function deficits but do not replace clinical judgment. Final determination integrates screening scores, interview findings, collateral information, and differential diagnosis to decide whether full diagnostic assessment and a tailored treatment plan are indicated."
  - heading: "Common Symptoms of Adult ADHD"
    body: "Adult ADHD manifests differently than in children and can present as chronic disorganization, difficulty with time management, impulsivity, and trouble maintaining focus. Many adults experience difficulties in workplace performance, relationships, and daily responsibilities. Key symptoms include forgetfulness, restlessness, emotional dysregulation, and difficulty prioritizing tasks. Recognizing these symptoms is the first step toward seeking appropriate evaluation and support."
  - heading: "Impact of ADHD on Adult Life"
    body: "Untreated ADHD can significantly impact an adult's personal relationships, career advancement, and overall well-being. Many adults struggle with low self-esteem due to years of unrecognized challenges. The condition can affect financial management, health behaviors, and mental health comorbidities such as anxiety and depression. Understanding the widespread impact of ADHD underscores the importance of screening and early intervention."
  - heading: "When to Seek Professional Evaluation"
    body: "If you consistently experience difficulty with focus, organization, or impulse control that affects your daily functioning, professional evaluation is recommended. Adults who had suspicions during childhood or who notice a pattern of difficulties should consider seeking assessment.
