  - heading: "What is Perceived Stress?"
    body: "Perceived stress is the subjective experience of how overwhelming, uncontrollable, or unpredictable someone judges the demands in their life to be, regardless of the objective number or severity of stressors; it reflects personal appraisal and coping resources and is what validated screening tools (like the Perceived Stress Scale) measure. High perceived stress is linked to changes in mood, behavior, sleep, and immune and cardiovascular function, and can predict both short-term symptoms (anxiety, poor concentration, irritability) and longer-term health risks (hypertension, metabolic issues, depression), while lower perceived stress generally indicates better psychological resilience and coping."
  - heading: "Understanding Your PSS-10 Score"
    body: "PSS-10 scores range from 0 to 40, with higher scores indicating greater perceived stress. Scores of 0-13 suggest low stress, 14-26 indicate moderate stress, and 27-40 reflect high perceived stress. Your score reflects how much stress you've experienced in the past month and how controllable you perceive life's challenges to be."

    body: "Chronic high stress is associated with numerous health problems including cardiovascular disease, weakened immunity, sleep disturbances, and mental health conditions. Understanding your stress level is essential for preventive health."
