  - heading: "Understanding Insomnia"
    body: "Insomnia is characterized by difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or obtaining refreshing sleep despite adequate opportunity. Acute insomnia is brief and often stress-related, while chronic insomnia persists for weeks or months. Insomnia affects daytime functioning, mood, and health. The ISI helps identify insomnia severity and guide treatment needs."
  - heading: "ISI Assessment Components"
    body: "The ISI measures severity of sleep onset problems (difficulty falling asleep), sleep maintenance issues (nighttime awakenings), early morning awakening (waking too early), and daytime consequences of poor sleep. It also assesses satisfaction with sleep quality and sleep interference with daily functioning. These dimensions comprehensively evaluate insomnia's impact."
  - heading: "Causes and Risk Factors for Insomnia"
    body: "Insomnia causes include stress, anxiety, depression, irregular sleep schedules, caffeine/alcohol use, and medical conditions. Sleep environment factors such as noise and light affect sleep quality. Age, gender, and life transitions increase insomnia risk. Identifying underlying causes helps guide appropriate treatment."
  - heading: "Sleep Hygiene and Self-Help Strategies"
    body: "Evidence-based sleep improvement strategies include consistent sleep schedules, a cool dark bedroom, limiting screen time before bed, and avoiding stimulants. Relaxation techniques such as progressive muscle relaxation and deep breathing improve sleep. Limiting naps and avoiding clock-watching help regulate sleep. These behavioral approaches are often effective for mild insomnia."
  - heading: "Professional Treatment for Insomnia"
    body: "For moderate to severe insomnia, cognitive-behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I) is the gold standard treatment. Sleep restriction therapy and stimulus control help reset sleep patterns. Medical evaluation rules out sleep disorders such as sleep apnea. Medication may be considered in combination with behavioral approaches. Professional support significantly improves sleep outcomes."
