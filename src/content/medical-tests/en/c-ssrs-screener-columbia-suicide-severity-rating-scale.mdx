    body: "Suicide risk involves the presence of suicidal thoughts (ideation) and the likelihood of acting on those thoughts. Risk factors include depression, hopelessness, previous suicide attempts, substance abuse, and access to means. Protective factors include social support, strong relationships, reasons for living, and access to mental health care. Suicide risk varies along a continuum requiring careful assessment."
  - heading: "C-SSRS Screening Components"
    body: "The C-SSRS screener assesses suicidal ideation (thoughts of killing oneself) and behavior (preparing for or attempting suicide). It evaluates the severity and frequency of thoughts and any associated behaviors. The tool helps clinicians identify individuals who require more comprehensive risk assessment and intervention. Positive screens warrant immediate professional evaluation."

    body: "Suicide warning signs include expressing suicidal thoughts, talking about being a burden, increased substance use, social withdrawal, and acquiring means of suicide. Major risk factors include mental health conditions (especially depression), previous suicide attempts, and access to lethal means. Recognizing these warning signs enables intervention."

    body: "Any suicidal thought or behavior should be taken seriously and evaluated by a mental health professional.
